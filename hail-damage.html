Identify the Damage

Signs Your Roof Has Hail Damage

Hail damage isn't always visible from the ground. Even small hailstones can cause significant damage that compromises your roof's integrity. Here's what our inspectors look for:

Dented or Bruised Shingles

Soft spots that feel spongy when pressed, often appearing as dark spots on the shingle surface.

Granule Loss

Bare spots on shingles where protective granules have been knocked off, often collecting in gutters.

Cracked or Split Shingles

Visible cracks running through shingles, which can allow water infiltration.

Dented Gutters & Downspouts

Metal components showing impact marks from hail, indicating your roof likely has damage too.

Damaged Flashing

Dents or cracks in the metal flashing around vents, chimneys, and skylights.

Damaged Siding & Windows

Hail marks on siding or cracked window screens suggest roof damage is likely.

Why Professional Inspection Matters

Hail damage is often invisible to the untrained eye but can reduce your roof's lifespan by years. Our certified inspectors know exactly where to look and how to document damage for insurance purposes.
